About

Shavian is an alternate writing system for English, where one sound corresponds to one letter, and vice versa (for the most part). It was published in 1962, and added to Unicode in April 2003.

The alphabet is partially featural, which means related sounds have related shapes, such as /p t k s/ being ğ‘ ğ‘‘ ğ‘’ ğ‘•, and their voiced versions, /b d g z/ being their flipped versions, ğ‘š ğ‘› ğ‘œ ğ‘Ÿ.
